The most important tips for protecting your online account:
Create complex passwords. The longer and more varied the password, the harder it is to crack. Do not use birth dates, first names, last names, or the nicknames of pets. Use extra security measures. Most often, users neglect to connect Google authentication and sms confirmation of login. However, this additionally adds the most impenetrable barrier to your security. Don't use other people's computers or mobile devices to enter your passwords. Also, don't use open networks in public places, as all traffic over them stays on the access point device. Don't reply to suspicious emails. Even if they came from friends on social networks or messengers. Scammers, these days, have learned to hack into social networking pages to send emails that, when opened, can infect your PC with malicious scripts that can send your personal data to criminals.
